The “Nicosiano Database” (NDB) is a publicly accessible document that has been created in order to assess not merely the presence of phonetic traits traceable to Northern dialects in the Nicosiano vocabulary, but rather the productivity of endogenous phonological rules. The NDB collects about 5,800 nouns and verbs, all in citation form, extracted from the Vocabolario del dialetto galloitalico di Nicosia e Sperlinga (VNiSp; Trovato / Menza 2020). Each of these lemmas plausibly represents a borrowing from Sicilian. Lemmas of clear patrimonial origin were excluded because it is the application of specifically Gallo-Italic phonological rules to Sicilian loanwords that constitutes evidence for the productivity of such rules (at least up to the time when a given loan entered Nicosiano and Sperlinghese), and allows to exclude the possibility that a given phonetic trait is present in Gallo-Italic only because it has been lexicalized. In the latter case, it would no longer be possible to argue for the preservation of Gallo-Italic phonology understood as the operation of synchronically active rules. The intensity of contact and the possible productivity of a given phenomenon can only be measured in light of phonological interference within borrowings from Sicilian or, possibly, from other varieties. On the basis of the available literature on the Gallo-Italic varieties of Nicosia and Sperlinga, a series of macro-phonological traits characteristic of the Northern repertoire was identified:


- Lenition of intervocalic voiceless consonants (stops and fricatives) [LENIZ];
- Nasalization of vowels [NASAL];
- Degemination [DEGEM];
- Assibilation [ASSIB];
- Maintenance of the clusters -MB- and -ND- [DISSIM];
- Non-metaphonic diphthongization [DITT];
- Maintenance of the Northern stressed seven-vowel system [VOCAL];
- Maintenance of Gallo-Italic unstressed vocalism [included in VOCAL];
- Apheresis of initial a- [AFER];
- Palatalization of stressed /a/ [PALAT];
- Elimination of Sicilian allophonic variants in weak position [ELIM];
- Deretroflexion of the outcomes of TR, TTR, and STR [DERETR];
- Weakening of sonorants [INDSON].


Each macro-trait was then subdivided into an intermediate level referred to as super-trait, and finally into a number of micro-traits, taking into account the orthographic conventions of both the source forms (Sicilian lemmas drawn from the Vocabolario Siciliano, VS) and the target forms (lemmas from the VNiSp). Each micro-trait was assigned a specific label in order to streamline the derivation process. The derivation mechanism begins with the Nicosiano (or Sperlinga) lemma from the VNiSp and correlates it with possible derivational candidates drawn from the VS. For each candidate, the relevant micro-traits are identified, and the derivation proceeds backward (i.e., from right to left; see column X) until the Nicosiano lemma is reached. Among the proposed derivations, the most plausible one is selected on the basis of qualitative analysis. Consider the following derivation: the Nicosiano verb segutè ‘pursue’ is correlated with Sicilian assicutàri:


segutè <-vocal.7.b-- sigutè <-palat.5-- sigutàri <-leniz.1-- sicutàri <-degem.13-- ssicutàri <-afer.1-- assicutàri


In the derivational process from assicutàri to segutè, we obtain a “raw” derivational string, which is independent of relative chronology, according to which the micro-trait afer.1 is first identified, yielding the intermediate form *ssicutàri. In the next step, micro-trait degem.13 produces *sicutàri, followed by leniz.1 (sigutàri), palat.5 (sigutè), and finally vocal.7.b (segutè). The outcomes of derivation can vary considerably (and must undergo qualitative analysis for adjustments). They range from “zero derivation”, which show no application of Gallo-Italic phonological rules with respect to the corresponding Sicilian lemmas (as in anciòva ‘anchovy’, fastùca ‘pistachio’, and sausìzza ‘sausage’), to very long derivations such as that of böfetìn ‘small table’ from Sicilian bbuffittìnu, displaying six distinct derivational steps.
